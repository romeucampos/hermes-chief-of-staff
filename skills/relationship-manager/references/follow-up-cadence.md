# Follow-up Cadence Reference

Default cadence (configurable in CHIEF_OF_STAFF_CONTEXT.md):

## Touch 1 — Day 2 (Gentle)

- **Purpose**: Light nudge, assumes the message was simply missed.
- **Tone**: Friendly, brief, no pressure.
- **Example**: "Just circling back on the below — let me know if you have any questions."

## Touch 2 — Day 5 (Direct)

- **Purpose**: Clear signal that a response is needed.
- **Tone**: Professional, direct, offers flexibility.
- **Example**: "Wanted to make sure this didn't slip through. Are you still interested in connecting? Happy to work around your schedule."

## Touch 3 — Day 7 (Final)

- **Purpose**: Last attempt before closing the loop.
- **Tone**: Respectful, gives an easy out.
- **Example**: "I'll leave this with you — no pressure. Feel free to reach out whenever the timing is right."

## After Touch 3

- Stop following up.
- Flag for the owner with a summary: who, what the original outreach was, what happened.
- The owner decides: one more personal attempt, different channel, or let it go.

## Cadence Adjustments

- **VIP contacts**: Tighter cadence (1 → 3 → 5 days) — these matter more.
- **Cold outreach**: Standard cadence, but be prepared for low response rates.
- **Warm introductions**: Extended patience (3 → 7 → 14 days) — referral relationships deserve more time.
- **Time-sensitive**: Same-day + next-day follow-up if there's a deadline.

## Tracking

- Record each touch with date and method (email, message, etc.).
- Record the response (or lack thereof) after each touch.
- When a response comes in at any point, the cadence resets.
